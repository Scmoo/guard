1 When This Policy Applies

This Privacy Policy applies to the Wound Guard portal and all associated online services owned or operated by Wound Guard that link to this policy (collectively, "Online Services"). It governs how we collect, use, store, and share information in connection with your use of the portal.

This policy applies to licensed healthcare providers, clinic administrators, and authorized staff members who access the Wound Guard portal on behalf of their organization. By accessing or using the Online Services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this policy.

This policy does not apply to information collected through other means, such as in-person interactions or telephone communications not directly tied to the Online Services. It also does not govern the privacy practices of any third-party websites or services that may be linked from the portal — their use is governed by their own privacy policies.

Provider & Clinic Accounts
All organizations accessing the Wound Guard portal execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) prior to gaining access. By using the platform, your organization confirms that a valid BAA is in place between your organization and Wound Guard.

2 Information We Collect

We collect two categories of information through the Online Services: information you provide directly to us, and information collected automatically through your use of the platform.

Information you provide directly:

  • Organization and provider account information, including name, email address, business address, National Provider Identifier (NPI), state license numbers, and specialty
  • Patient information submitted when placing orders, including patient name, date of birth, insurance information, diagnosis codes, and wound care history
  • Order details including products selected, shipping destination, billing codes, and required documentation
  • Communications you send to Wound Guard through the portal, including support requests and account inquiries

Information collected automatically:

  • Login timestamps, session duration, and pages visited within the portal
  • Device type, operating system, and web browser information
  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Actions taken within the portal, including orders placed, notifications viewed, and records modified
Patient Data
Patient information submitted through the portal is considered Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA. Wound Guard handles all PHI in accordance with its obligations as a HIPAA Business Associate and in compliance with the HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules.

3 How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Processing, fulfilling, and tracking wound care product orders placed through the portal
  • Verifying patient insurance eligibility and coordinating authorization where required
  • Sending order status updates and notifications through the portal and via email
  • Authenticating user accounts and maintaining session security
  • Responding to support requests and communications from authorized portal users
  • Maintaining records of transactions and communications as required by law
  • Improving the platform's functionality, performance, and user experience
  • Detecting, investigating, and preventing unauthorized access or fraudulent activity
  • Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and legal process, including HIPAA
  • Protecting the rights, safety, and welfare of patients, providers, and Wound Guard

We will not use your information for purposes materially different from those described above without first notifying you and, where required, obtaining your explicit consent.

4 How We Share Your Information

Wound Guard does not sell your information or the information of your patients to any third party. We do not share information with advertisers. We share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Subprocessors and vendors: We share information with third-party service providers who process data on our behalf, including our database infrastructure provider, email delivery services, and authentication provider. All subprocessors who handle PHI are required to execute a Business Associate Agreement with Wound Guard prior to accessing any data
  • Fulfillment and shipping partners: Order and shipping information is shared with suppliers and carriers solely for the purpose of fulfilling orders placed through the portal
  • Insurance verification services: Patient insurance information may be shared with eligibility verification services as part of the order workflow
  • Legal compliance: We may disclose information in response to a valid court order, subpoena, or government request, or as otherwise required by law
  • Business transfers: If Wound Guard is acquired, merged, or undergoes a similar transaction, information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to the same privacy protections described in this policy
  • Safety: We may share information where necessary to protect the health, safety, or welfare of a patient or other individual
We Do Not Sell Data
Wound Guard does not sell, rent, or trade your information or patient PHI to any third party for marketing, advertising, or any commercial purpose.

5 HIPAA & Business Associates

Wound Guard operates as a Business Associate under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations. As a Business Associate, Wound Guard is required to:

  • Use and disclose PHI only as permitted by its Business Associate Agreements and applicable law
  • Implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI
  • Report breaches of unsecured PHI to the applicable Covered Entity in accordance with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule
  • Ensure that any subcontractors who handle PHI on Wound Guard's behalf agree to the same restrictions through their own Business Associate Agreements

The clinics and healthcare providers using the Wound Guard portal are Covered Entities under HIPAA. They maintain the direct patient relationship and are responsible for obtaining appropriate patient authorizations for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations. Wound Guard's access to patient PHI is limited to what is necessary to fulfill its role in the ordering workflow.

BAA Requirement
All organizations must execute a Business Associate Agreement with Wound Guard before accessing the portal. Wound Guard also maintains BAAs with all subprocessors who handle PHI on its behalf, including its database and infrastructure provider.

6 Role-Based Access & Audit Logging

Access to information within the Wound Guard portal is controlled through a role-based permissions system. This means that each user account is assigned a role that determines which records and functions that user can access. For example, a provider may access only patient records and orders associated with their own organization, while an administrator may have broader access within that organization.

No user can access the data of another organization. Data is isolated at the organization level within the platform, and this isolation is enforced at the database level — not solely at the application level.

The platform maintains audit logs that record who accessed which records, when, and what actions were taken. These logs are retained in accordance with HIPAA requirements and may be used to investigate unauthorized access or to demonstrate compliance in the event of an audit.

7 Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, subject to longer retention periods required by law.

  • Order records and PHI: Retained for a minimum of six (6) years from the date of creation or the date it was last in effect, in accordance with HIPAA's documentation retention requirements
  • Account information: Retained for the duration of the active account relationship and for a reasonable period thereafter as required for legal and compliance purposes
  • Audit logs: Retained for a minimum of six (6) years in accordance with the HIPAA Security Rule
  • Automatically collected data: Retained for a period necessary to support platform operations, security monitoring, and compliance obligations

When information is no longer required to be retained, it is securely deleted or de-identified in accordance with applicable standards.

8 Cookies & Tracking

The Wound Guard portal uses cookies and similar technologies solely for the purpose of maintaining user sessions and ensuring platform security. We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking pixels, or behavioral analytics tools that share your activity with outside parties.

Session cookies are placed on your device when you log in and are used to keep you authenticated during your session. These cookies expire when you log out or close your browser.

Persistent cookies may be used to remember your preferences within the portal, such as sidebar state or display settings. These do not contain PHI and are not shared with third parties.

You may disable cookies through your browser settings at any time; however, doing so may prevent you from logging in or using core features of the portal. We do not currently respond to browser "Do Not Track" signals, as there is no universal standard for how such signals should be interpreted.

9 Protecting Your Information

Wound Guard maintains administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to protect the information you provide and that we collect. These safeguards include:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS (Transport Layer Security)
  • Encryption of data at rest within our database infrastructure
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel only
  • Multi-factor authentication for portal accounts
  • Regular security monitoring and audit logging
  • Network isolation of production database environments

While we implement these safeguards, no system can guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your login credentials and for logging out of the portal when your session is complete, particularly on shared or public devices. Wound Guard is not liable for unauthorized access resulting from your failure to secure your account credentials or devices.

10 Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured Protected Health Information, Wound Guard will fulfill its breach notification obligations as a Business Associate under the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule:

  • Wound Guard will notify the applicable Covered Entity (the clinic or provider organization) without unreasonable delay and no later than sixty (60) calendar days after discovery of the breach
  • The notification will include, to the extent possible, the nature of the PHI involved, the unauthorized persons involved, whether the PHI was actually acquired or viewed, and the extent to which the risk of harm has been mitigated
  • The Covered Entity is responsible for notifying affected individuals and, where required, the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and applicable media outlets, in accordance with their own HIPAA obligations
  • Wound Guard will cooperate fully with the Covered Entity in its breach notification and remediation obligations

11 Your Rights

Authorized users of the Wound Guard portal have the following rights with respect to their account information:

  • Access: You may request a copy of the account and usage information we hold about you
  • Correction: You may request that inaccurate information in your account be corrected
  • Deletion: You may request deletion of your account information, subject to our legal obligation to retain certain records as described in the Data Retention section above
  • Communication preferences: You may manage your notification and email preferences within the portal account settings

Patient rights with respect to their PHI are governed by the HIPAA Privacy Rule and are administered by the Covered Entity (the clinic or provider organization) — not directly by Wound Guard. Patients should direct PHI access requests to their healthcare provider.

To exercise your account rights, contact us at privacy@woundguard.com. We will acknowledge your request within ten (10) business days and respond within forty-five (45) days, or notify you if additional time is required.

13 California Residents

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). However, please note that much of the information handled through the Wound Guard portal constitutes PHI governed by HIPAA and CMIA, which are exempt from certain CCPA obligations.

For information that is subject to CCPA, California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell
  • Request deletion of personal information we hold about them
  • Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information — note that Wound Guard does not sell personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising their CCPA rights

To submit a California privacy request, contact us at privacy@woundguard.com with "California Privacy Request" in the subject line. We will respond within forty-five (45) days of receipt.
